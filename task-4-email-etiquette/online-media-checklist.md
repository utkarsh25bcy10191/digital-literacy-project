The "Do’s" (Best Practices)

-> Audit Your Privacy Settings: Regularly check your "Who can see my posts" settings on platforms like Instagram and Facebook to ensure personal content isn't visible to the entire public.

-> Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always use an extra layer of security (OTP or Authenticator app) to prevent unauthorized access, even if your password is leaked.

-> Think Before You Post: Ask yourself if a recruiter or professor would be okay seeing this post 5 years from now. If the answer is "no," don't post it.

-> Fact-Check Before Sharing: In an era of deepfakes and misinformation, verify news from official sources before hitting the 'Share' button to avoid spreading panic or lies.

-> Build a Professional Brand: Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects, certifications, and skills to connect with industry experts early in your B.Tech journey.

The "Don’ts" (Risky Behaviors)

-> Don’t Share Sensitive Data: Never post photos of your College ID, Registration Number, Hostel Room number, or Aadhaar card. This info is gold for identity thieves.

-> Don’t Overshare Your Location: Avoid "Live" check-ins or tagging your exact location while you are still there. It's safer to post travel photos after you have left the venue.

-> Don’t Engage with Trolls: Avoid heated arguments or "flame wars" with anonymous accounts. It reflects poorly on your digital footprint and can lead to cyberbullying.

-> Don’t Use Single Passwords: Never use the same password for your social media as you do for your VTOP or official college email.

-> Don’t Post Offensive Content: Avoid sharing memes or comments that are discriminatory, hateful, or could violate the university’s code of conduct.
