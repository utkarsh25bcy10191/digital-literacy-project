Overview

This is a story about a person, Ravi Sharma, who is a college student. He got caught up in a phishing scam. This story shows how bad people use tricks to get information from people who are good with technology.

The Incident:

Ravi got an email that looked exactly like it came from his bank. The email said he had to do something away. So he clicked on a link. Gave them his login information and a special code. This was a mistake. The bad person was able to lock Ravi out of his account take his money and even get a loan in his name.

Key Lessons:

What we learn from this story is that bad people can make things look normal. They try to make you do things quickly so you do not think about it. The important thing to remember is that being safe is not just about the software you use. It is about being careful and checking things before you do them.

Prevention Checklist:

- Never click on links in emails that ask for your login information or personal stuff.

- Always go to your banks website by typing the address in yourself.

- Remember that real banks will never ask for your code by email.

- Check the website address to make sure it is spelled right and does not have any characters.

- Turn on two-step verification and alerts for when something happens with your accounts.

What happened to Ravi Sharma shows that one little mistake can cause a lot of problems. If people just take a second to think before they do something they can avoid scams. Ravi Sharmas experience with phishing and identity theft and phishing is a lesson for everyone to be more careful, with phishing and identity theft.
